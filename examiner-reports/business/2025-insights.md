## Common errors by question type

### Section 1 — Short-response questions (10 marks each, compulsory)

The marking scheme uses three template patterns across the 12 questions:

- **`5@ 3,2,2,2,1`** (Q1, Q3, Q4) — five matching/multiple-choice items, descending value
- **`(3+2) (3+2)`** (Q5, Q6, Q8, Q9, Q10, Q12) — two developed points, each Name (3) + Explain (2)
- **`(a) 3+3 (b) 2+2`** (Q2, Q11) — definition split + two examples / two items
- **`(a) 3+2 (b) 5@1`** (Q7) — definition (3+2) + five marking points × 1m each (the "show your workings" template)

The Q7 ratio template is unusual: 5 distinct items each worth 1 mark. For Debt/Equity, the five marking points are: identifying Debt (€300,000), identifying Equity (Issued Share Capital + Reserves = €600,000), substituting into the ratio, calculating the result (0.5), expressing as a ratio (0.5:1). Stating "0.5:1" without working caps you at 1 mark.

### Section 2 — Applied Business Question (Inis Bia)

The ABQ template is consistent across years and is what the 2015 Chief Examiner's Report flags as the most punitive question on the paper.

- **Part (A) Outline entrepreneurial skills:** `4@5 (2+2+1)` — Name (2) + Explain (2) + **Link (1)**
- **Part (B)(i) Evaluate management control:** `4@7 (1+2+1+3)` — Name (1) + Explain (2) + Link (1) + **Evaluate effectiveness (3)** — evaluation is the most heavily weighted single component
- **Part (B)(ii) Recommend one improvement:** `1@2m`
- **Part (C) Describe HRM functions:** `5@6 (2+2+2)` — Name (2) + Explain (2) + **Link (2)** — link weighted heaviest at 2/6

Marking-scheme rule (verbatim, p.6):
> *"In relation to the links given, they must be a direct relevant quote/phrase/statement from the ABQ indicating that candidates clearly understand the point(s) of theory presented. Separate links are required in each section. No link awarded without relevant theory."*

The "no link awarded without relevant theory" line is decisive. A student who writes a perfect quote from the case but no theory scores 0 for that point. The reverse (perfect theory, no link) also scores 0 for the link mark.

### Section 3 — Extended response (60 marks per question, 4 of 8 attempted)

Most Section 3 sub-parts use the **`4@5 (Name, Explain, Link/Example/Reference)`** template, with sub-templates that vary by cue:

- **`4@5 (4+1)(4+1)(3+2)(3+2) Explain + Example`** — used when the cue is "Illustrate" with a list of named items (e.g. Q1A contract elements, Q3C trade barriers, Q4A contract termination). Notable: examples worth 1 mark for the first two items, 2 marks for the last two.
- **`4@5 (2+3) Name + Explain`** — used for "Discuss" with no example required (e.g. Q5A communication factors, Q5B SWOT, Q6B managing change, Q7A acquisition advantages/disadvantages — for which scheme requires "2 of each" advantages and disadvantages)
- **`4@5 (2+2+1) Name + Explain + Example/Reference`** — used for "Explain using examples" or "Outline" with examples (e.g. Q1B industrial action, Q2C government climate creation, Q3B global marketing mix)

### Section 3 Q1(C) — the "List" rule

Q1(C)(ii) marking grid: `8 marks (3, 3, 1, 1)` for "List four grounds." The first two grounds named earn 3 marks each; the next two earn 1 mark each. This means:

- Naming **fewer than 4** caps you at 3+3+1 = 7 (or fewer)
- Writing paragraphs explaining the grounds is wasted — the cue is "List", explanation earns nothing extra

The 2015 Chief Examiner's Report flagged this exact pattern: *"some candidates wrote paragraphs to explain the protected grounds rather than just naming the grounds, as was required in the question"* (p.17).

### Section 3 Q8(C) — the breakeven chart

Marking grid:
- **With chart drawn:** Title 2m, Axes 1+1m, Fixed Costs line 3m, Total Costs line 3m, Total Revenue line 3m, BEP marked 4m, Profit at forecast output marked 4m, Margin of Safety marked 4m = 25m total
- **Calculations only (no chart):** BEP 4m + Profit 4m + MoS 4m = **12m maximum**

So a student who only does the calculations — even perfectly — caps at 48% of the available marks. The chart is the majority of the grade.

Within the chart: missing the title costs 2m. Missing axis labels costs 2m (1m per axis). The marking is granular and unforgiving.

## Strategic / structural observations

- **Question cues drive the mark grid.** "Illustrate" requires examples (`(4+1)`-style sub-marks). "Discuss" requires distinct points (`(2+3)`). "List" rewards brevity only. "Define" wants a precise sentence. "Outline" expects developed points but no examples.
- **The ABQ link mark is non-substitutable.** No amount of theory makes up for missing the case-link. No amount of case-link makes up for missing the theory.
- **"Show your workings" is enforced via the marking grid, not via prose instruction.** Section 1 Q7's `5@1m` scheme means each procedural step is its own marking point. Skip the substitution and you skip a mark, even with the right answer.
- **Numerical questions have multi-stage marking:** Q6(C) Gemma's tax computation awards individual ticks for each line of the calculation (`❶`). The grid treats each rate slab, each tax credit, each total as a distinct mark.
- **Marks-to-minutes:** 400 marks / 180 mins = 0.45 mins/mark. So 10m short response = ~4.5 mins; 20m sub-part = ~9 mins; 60m Section 3 question = ~27 mins; 80m ABQ = ~36 mins.

## Misconceptions

- **Authorised vs Issued share capital** — the Q7 Debt/Equity calculation gives both numbers. Authorised (€900,000) is the maximum that can be issued; Equity for the ratio is **Issued (€550,000) + Reserves (€50,000)** = €600,000. Using Authorised gives 300/950 = 0.32 — wrong.
- **ABQ as comprehension piece** — rewriting case material instead of applying theory (the canonical ABQ failure mode, called out in 2015 CER).
- **Skipping evaluation in B(i)** — the marking grid weights evaluation at 3/7 marks. Many students name and explain the control type with a link, then stop short of judging effectiveness.
- **Listing without limit** — Q1(C)(ii) marking grid is `(3, 3, 1, 1)`. Listing 6+ grounds doesn't earn extra marks; the first 4 are the only ones counted.
- **"Calculations only" on breakeven** — capping at 12/25. Most students who do only calculations don't realise the chart is 13 of the 25 marks.
- **Diagram without labels / title** — universal across charts. Q8(C) breakeven, Q4 bar charts, Q5 product life cycle. Same pattern as Geography's "diagram without labels = 0".
